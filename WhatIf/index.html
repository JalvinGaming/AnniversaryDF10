What If?


One million years we lived in the woods
The flowers can fill us with hope
The deers and rabbits and squirrels could
Give us friends when others said “nope.”


But then you had bears and these creatures would roar
And chase us down exciting us,
But all of that hope and excitement turned into bore
Of the cities of train and bus.


One night I left home to venture outside
Encountered a gate, “What’s hiding behind?”
My brain went haywire, but all hope was lost,
When all that I saw was grey.


All life was gone, the black roads were empty,
The places for people were gone.
When I did see a person, they were in a metal box,
And I wish, oh I wish they were back in the forest.


Seeking hope, I returned to the forest,
But the gate was nowhere to be found.
I looked around, but no luck, but I know,
I think we can turn it around.


Bring back the flowers, bring back the fauna,
Seek all the forests and break from the sauna,
We need some bears, we need some cliffs,
But all that is just a “What if?”
